Understanding your bill (Cont'd)
Merchant Function Charge: A charge that recovers costs associated with procuring electricity, procuring and storing natural gas, conducting credit and collections activities, and managing uncollectible
accounts.
Monthly rate adjustment: Adjustment for miscellaneous costs and credits, and from October through May, for the effect of variation from normal weather.
New Read: Monthly meter readings are no longer used for billing purposes. Instead, we use the data provided by your smart meter(s), which improve accuracy by recording your energy use in 15-minute
intervals. Your New Read is provided for illustrative purposes only.
Prior Read: Your prior meter reading was calculated when your last bill was issued.
Reading Difference: The reading difference is the result of subtracting your prior meter reading from your new meter reading. Please note: the New Read and Prior Read values shown on your bill have
been rounded and/or truncated. Calculating the Read Diff yourself may result in a different value than the one shown here.
Reading Type: You will see the word  Estimated  here if your smart meter was unable to communicate your energy use during the billing period. Otherwise, your reading type will be  Actual .
Remaining: Charge for maintaining the system through which Con Edison delivers gas to you.
Sales tax: Tax collected on behalf of New York State and/or your locality.
System Benefits Charge: A charge that recovers costs associated withøenergy-efficiency and renewable-energy programs implemented by the Company and the New York State Energy Research and
Development Authority (NYSERDA).
Temperature * Source: Central Park Weather station.
Total Usage (kWh): Your total usage is the sum of the 15-minute interval data recorded by your smart meter(s) during the billing period. This is used to calculate the energy-delivery and energy-supply
charges on your bill.
Con Edison proposes 
investment plan to 
maintain safe, reliable 
service and enhance 
support for customers 
Consolidated Edison Company of New York  
(Con Edison) is seeking state approval for  
infrastructure investments that reflect our  
commitment to our customers to build a clean,  
reliable and resilient grid of the future. 
The investment plan will support our efforts to  
maintain safe and reliable gas service and the most 
reliable electric service in the nation as we meet  
customer demand for clean energy options. 
Affordability is a top priority for Con Edison. The plan 
includes measures to enhance customer affordability 
programs and improve IT infrastructure and tools, 
making it easier to do business with us. 
The filing requests new rates for one year starting on 
January 1, 2026. Con Edison is open to a multi-year 
settlement, which would provide more cost certainty 
for our customers. Con Edison estimates that its 
investments would require approximately  
$1.6 billion more in electric revenue and about  
$440 million more in gas revenue. This would mean 
an average electric bill increase of roughly 11 percent 
and a gas bill increase of 13 percent.
