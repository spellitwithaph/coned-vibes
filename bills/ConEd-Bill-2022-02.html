Understanding your bill
Basic service charge (Electric): Charge for basic system infrastructure and customer-related services, including customer accounting, and metering services.

Billing charges: These charges are for the electricity and gas you need (supply) and getting the electricity and gas to you (delivery). Rates are based on a 30 day period. When your billing period is more
or less than 30 days, we prorate your bill accordingly.

Understanding your bill (Cont'd)
Delivery: Charge for maintaining the system through which Con Edison delivers electricity to you.
Gas Supply: Charge for the gas supplied to you by Con Edison.
GRT & other tax surcharges: Taxes on Con Edison gross receipts from sales of utility services and other tax surcharges.
Merchant Function Charge (Electric and Gas): Charge associated with procuring electricity and storing natural gas, credit and collection related activities and uncollectible accounts.
Monthly rate adjustment: Adjustment for miscellaneous costs and credits, and from October through May, for the effect of variation from normal weather.
Remaining: Charge for maintaining the system through which Con Edison delivers gas to you.
Sales tax: Tax collected on behalf of New York City.
System Benefit Charge (Electric): The System Benefits Charge recovers costs associated with clean energy activities conducted by the New York State Energy Research and Development Authority
(NYSERDA) and energy efficiency programs implemented by the Company.
System Benefit Charge (Gas): The System Benefits Charge recovers costs associated with  energy efficiency programs implemented by the Company and the New York State Research and Development
Authority (NYSERDA).

We Have Filed 
Electric, Gas Rate 
Proposals for 2023
On January 28, 2022, Con Edison filed a request 
for new electric and gas rates with the New York 
State Pub Service Commission (PSC) beginning in 
January 2023.
These rate requests will help fund our Clean Energy 
Commitment in support of New York State’s climate 
goals and enable us to strengthen and upgrade  
our infrastructure. They will also give customers  
convenient access to energy-saving products, 
enhance safety and reliability, and make it easier for 
customers to get information about their energy use.
Our electric rate proposal calls for an increase of 
$1.2 billion, and our gas rate proposal seeks an 
increase of $500 million. For more information, visit 
conEd.com/AboutRates. We are open to exploring  
multiyear plans for both electric and gas service 
with the PSC, which could result in lower annual 
increases.
The Commission may approve, modify, or reject any 
or all of the proposed tariff changes. Among other 
things, the Commission may require revisions of 
the proposed amount of the increases appable 
to particular classifications of service, or changes in 
rates appable to those classifications for which no 
increase is proposed by the company.
Learn more under Annual Reports on ConEdison.com.

Propuestas para  
tarifas de electricidad 
y gas para 2023
El 28 de enero de 2022, Con Edison presentó ante la 
Comisión de Servicios Púbos (PSC) del estado de 
Nueva York una soitud para fijar nuevas tarifas de 
electricidad y gas a partir de enero de 2023.
Estas nuevas tarifas ayudarán a financiar nuestro 
compromiso con la energía limpia en apoyo de los 
objetivos climáticos del estado de Nueva York y nos 
permitirán reforzar y mejorar nuestra infraestructura. 
También ofrecerán a los clientes un acceso cómodo 
a productos de bajo consumo energético, mejorarán 
la seguridad y confiabilidad, y facilitarán la obtención 
de información sobre su consumo de energía.
Nuestra propuesta prevé un aumento de $1.2 mil 
millones para las tarifas de electricidad y un aumento 
de $500 millones para las tarifas de gas. Para conocer 
más, visite conEd.com/AboutRates. Estamos abiertos  
a explorar planes de tarifas plurianuales para el  
servicio de electricidad y gas, con la PSC, lo que 
conduciría a menores aumentos anuales.
La PSC podrá aprobar, modificar o rechazar  
cualquiera o todos los cambios de tarifas propuestos. 
La PSC puede requerir revisiones a la cantidad 
propuesta para los aumentos apables a ciertas 
clasificaciones de servicio, o cambios en las tarifas 
apables a aquellas clasificaciones para las cuales la 
compañía no propone ningún aumento. 
Para más información, consulte la sección Informes 
anuales en conEdison.com.
